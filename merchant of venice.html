The Merchant Of Venice

Introduction
The Merchant Of Venice is a romantic comedy written by the Bard of Avon Willam Shakespeare. It revolves around the main characters of the play- Antonio, Bassanio, Portia and Shylock. Other characters are= Salarino, Salanio. Nerissa, Jessica, Gratiano and Launcelot Gobbo.

Plot Summary
The play starts with the central character Antonio who is accompanified by his friends Salanio and Salarino. Antonio is melancholic and Salanio and Salarino try to guess the reason behind Antonio's melancholyness. They first guess that Antonio is sad because he is worried about his ships at sea but Antonio denies it. They then guess that Antonio is sad because he is in love but Antonio denies that too. Then we are introduced to another central character of he play, Bassanio. Bassanio is Antonio's best friend and is a spendrift.

Antonio tells Bassanio that he does not have the money at the moment. He tells Bassanio to ask for a loan with his name. Bassanio goes to ask for a loan to Shylock, a cruel Jew moneylender who had many grudges against Antonio. Shylock at first refuses but later agrees to give the loan of 3000 ducats. Antonio and Shylock sign a bond which states that if Antonio doesn's pay the loan in 3 months without any interest then Shylock will take a pound of flesh from Antonio's body. Antonio gives the loan to Bassanio. Meanwhile Lorenzo who is in love with Jessica, Shylock's daughter makes a plan ro run away with Jessica.

The plan of Lorenzo succeeds and Shylock gets more angry as her daughter ran away with a Christian. Meanwhile, the due date for paying back the loan comes near and Shylock hires a lawyer as there are rumours that Antonio's ships are broken down. On the due date, Antonio fails to repay Shylock who files a case against him to the Duke. The Duke tries to stop Shylock.

Meanwhile Bassanio is married to Portia and comes to know about Antonio. He takes money from Potia and rushes to Venice. Portia and Nerissa become fake lawyers and go to Venice without telling Bassanio and Gratiano. In the court, Bassanio offers Shylock to pay 6000 ducats but Shylock denies it. Portia, disguised as a lawyer imposes charges on Shylock for threatening to cut down a flesh from Antonio's body. Shylock discharges the case and then Bassanio takes Antonio to Belmont. Antonio comes to know that his ships are not wrecked.

The End
